What is a BCI?

A BCI (Or Brain-Computer Interface) is a method for mapping the neurological activity of the CNS (Central Nervous System) to an artificial system which then takes decisions based on the measured CNS activity.
BCIs are sometimes referred to as BMIs (Brain-Machine Interface).

How can we use a BCI?

The potentials of BCIs have yet to be fully explored, however applications of BCIs are already emerging from research laboratories globally.
At USF (University of South Florida), the director of the Neuro-Machine Interaction Lab, Dr. Marvin Andujar, has been developing technology that allows people to create digital art by thinking about colours or shapes.
At face value, this may not seem very useful for the advancement of humanity, but the primary goal of the project is to allow people with ADHD (Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder) to essentially train their focusing abilities.
